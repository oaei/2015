Global Evaluation Results

Summary table showing the averaged precision/recall/f-measure results for the whole set of queries: AML, DKPAOM, LogMap, LogMapC and XMap were the only matchers whose alignments allowed to answer all the queries of the evaluation.

AML was the best performing tool for what concerns averaged precision (same value of XMap), recall (same value of LogMap) and f-measure, closely followed by LogMap, LogMapC and XMap.

The difference in results between the publicly available reference alignment of Conference track (RA1) and its repaired version (RAR1, not to be confused with RA2 of Conference track!) was not significant. The f-measure ranking between the two reference alignments is almost totally preserved, at the exception of Lily, which is ranked 11-th w.r.t. RA1, and 9-th w.r.t. RAR1 (improving its results w.r.t. JarvisOM and LogMapLite).

Evaluation Results Aggregated (by query family)

Basic queries were sufficiently (f-measure of at least 0.6) answered by half of the matchers, and only GMap and RSDLWB did not reach an f-measure score of at least 0.5, due to logical violations that prevented query answering in most cases. Concerning advanced queries, the best result has been obtained by Mamba matcher, which achieved an f-measure of 0.533, followed by AML, LogMap and LogMapC, with a score of 0.333. All the other matchers achieved an f-measure less than 0.3, with 6 matchers which totally failed all queries (vastly due to unsatisfiability of the aligned ontology). Surprisingly, this year the best global results have been achieved for violations queries, that has been correctly covered by AML, DKPAOM, LogMap, COMMAND, LogMapC and XMAP, in decreasing order of f-measure w.r.t. RA1. Notably, DKPAOM achieved an impressive f-measure of 0.999 w.r.t. RAR1, showing an effective handling of logical violations.
